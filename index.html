Tapasya runs three progressively sophisticated training regimes on your text corpus — byte-level minimal, byte-level with more compute, and BPE-tokenized — using only forward passes (no backprop). Each stage's output motivates the next stage's addition.

The algorithm is EGGROLL (evolution strategies) by Isaac 2025. Your corpus never leaves the tab.

Running on CPU — training will be slow (WebGPU unavailable in this browser).

Stage 1 — Byte-level, minimal idle
This is the smallest thing that works. A 4-layer transformer with 130,000 parameters, trained on your corpus as raw bytes — vocab size 256, no tokenizer. We run it for 500 steps, about 2 minutes. Expect output that recognizably tries to be your corpus's language but fails at word boundaries.
